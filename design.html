MERCURY

THE BRAND

The visual identity of our brand embraces the concepts of femininity, power and expression. Our colour palette, typography and overall website design all align with our young, creative female target audience and the brand identity outlined in our Brand Road Map. We selected a colour palette that stands behind our brand meaning, which is about female empowerment and speaking your mind/self-expression. Since the standard colours assigned to women are often pink-ish colours and red overtones, we chose these two colours as a base to build an aesthetic that might seem as stereotypical and an acknowledged colour palette by women; but women speaking their minds are fighting this. In our brand context, pink and red stand for being strong, feeling empowered and being proud of your gender. A creative outlet. For the visual aesthetic of our website, we chose to use very bright, lively and enthusiastic women. Most of the women portrayed in the promotional pictures on this website are diverse and fighting those stereotypes, women of different colour, different skintypes and hairstyles. But still powerful women, nonetheless.

To maintain consistency across all platforms, red and pink are mainly used within promotional pictures, the website style and logo-wise. And although our brand vision involves women empowerment, it is not only for that purpose. It is about sexuality empowerment, gender empowerment and all these different lifestyles that bring women together to create. We used soft pastel colours for the background and tried a minimal style for a peaceful and relaxing feeling. We do not want our audience to feel stressed or uncomfortable, this is a safe space created for our audience to express themselves in any way they feel comfortable to do so.

THE DESIGN

Fonts used: "Gill Sans", "Gill Sans MT", "Myriad Pro", "DejaVu Sans Condensed", Helvetica, Arial, "sans-serif", Montserrat Extra Bold, Montserrat Extra Light. We opted for a very minimalistic typography so that the design of the text does not distract from the rest of the content.

Color Choices: Main colours we used are Deep Red for, light pink and light beige. As explained, these are colours correlated with femininity, because of our female target group.
